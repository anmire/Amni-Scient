PRIVACY POLICY

Effective Date: March 3, 2026  |  Last Updated: March 3, 2026

This privacy policy describes how software products developed and published by Amni-Scient ("we", "us", "our") handle user information. This policy covers the following products:

  • Amni-Crypt — File encryption application (Android)
  • Amni-Haven — Private chat client (Android)
  • Amni-AI — Local intelligence engine (Desktop)
  • Amni-Core — Systems platform (Desktop/Embedded)

1. DATA WE COLLECT

We collect no personal data across any of our products. No product requires account creation, registration, login credentials, email addresses, phone numbers, names, or location data. We do not operate servers that receive, store, or process user information.

2. PRODUCT-SPECIFIC DATA HANDLING

2.1 AMNI-CRYPT

All encryption and decryption operations execute entirely on your device. No files, passphrases, encryption keys, cipher seals, QR codes, or user content ever leaves your device via our application. The app uses Android's scoped storage model, accessing only files you explicitly select through the system file picker. Encrypted output (.acrypt files) is saved to your Downloads directory. The built-in QR scanner uses your camera only when explicitly opened; frames are processed on-device in real-time and immediately discarded.

2.2 AMNI-HAVEN

Amni-Haven is a client application that connects to user-operated Haven servers. Message content, authentication tokens, and channel data are transmitted between your device and the server you choose to connect to. We do not operate, control, or have access to any Haven servers. Your server administrator controls all message data. JWT authentication tokens are stored locally on your device. Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) tokens are registered with Google's push notification service and your chosen server for push delivery; we do not receive or store these tokens.

2.3 AMNI-AI

Amni-AI runs entirely on your local machine. All inference, knowledge storage, self-improvement cycles, and model operations execute on your hardware using your GPU and local storage. No queries, responses, learning data, or model state are transmitted externally. The Dictionary Atlas and all associated texture data reside on your local disk and VRAM exclusively.

2.4 AMNI-CORE

Amni-Core is a systems platform that operates at the hardware level. It collects no telemetry, usage data, crash reports, or diagnostic information. No data is transmitted to Amni-Scient or any third party. All system operations, storage management, and computational tasks execute locally on the hardware where it is installed.

3.5 Third-Party Services (Amni-AI, Amni-Core)

Amni-AI and Amni-Core do not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or telemetry services. These products operate entirely offline once installed.

4. DATA SHARING

We do not sell, trade, share, or transfer any user data to third parties. The only third-party data processing occurs through Google AdMob and Firebase as described in Section 3, governed by Google's privacy policy.

5. DATA RETENTION

6. SECURITY

6.1 AMNI-CRYPT SECURITY

Amni-Crypt implements a proprietary cipher architecture that differs from conventional encryption software in several fundamental ways:

  • Extended block width: The cipher operates on 1024-byte (8192-bit) blocks, substantially wider than the 128-bit blocks used by AES and other standard ciphers. This expanded block size increases the internal state space and provides resistance against block-level pattern analysis.
  • Dual-stream entanglement: Unlike single-stream ciphers, Amni-Crypt processes two parallel data streams that co-evolve through identical nonlinear and linear transformations. The interaction between these streams at each round creates a cryptographically entangled state where attacking one stream requires simultaneous knowledge of the other, effectively doubling the state space an adversary must explore.
  • Proprietary round structure: Each of the 16 encryption rounds applies a unique sequence of fused nonlinear substitution, position-dependent permutation, and multi-lane linear diffusion layers. The specific composition, ordering, and parametric scheduling of these layers constitute proprietary intellectual property.
  • High-iteration key derivation: User passphrases undergo 600,000 rounds of key stretching with per-operation random salt, producing a 512-bit derived key. This exceeds OWASP recommended minimums by an order of magnitude, providing substantial resistance to brute-force and dictionary attacks.
  • Post-quantum shadow seeding: The secondary data stream is initialized using a NIST-standardized extendable output function from the SHA-3 family, ensuring the shadow state is unique per encryption and derived from a function resistant to known quantum computing attack vectors.
  • Authenticated encryption: Every encrypted container includes an integrity tag computed over the full ciphertext. Tampering, corruption, or incorrect passphrases are detected before decryption begins, preventing oracle attacks and ensuring data authenticity.
  • Zero key persistence: No encryption keys, derived keys, or passphrases are stored by the application after an operation completes. Key material exists only in volatile memory during active operations and is released immediately upon completion.

All cryptographic operations execute locally on the user's device. The cipher engine, round structure, table construction methodology, and stream interaction protocol are proprietary and protected as trade secrets under applicable intellectual property law.

6.2 AMNI-HAVEN SECURITY

Amni-Haven supports TLS/SSL connections to Haven servers, including self-signed certificate trust for private deployments. JWT authentication tokens are stored in the application's private storage, inaccessible to other applications under Android's sandboxing model. WebRTC voice and video calls establish peer-to-peer connections with DTLS encryption where network topology permits. All authentication credentials are stored locally and never transmitted to Amni-Scient.

6.3 AMNI-AI SECURITY

All model inference, knowledge retrieval, and self-improvement operations execute on local hardware. The Dictionary Atlas resides on local storage and VRAM. No queries or generated content are transmitted externally. Internal integrity is enforced via cryptographic hash seals on protected system files, ensuring core safety axioms cannot be modified by the self-improvement subsystem.

6.4 AMNI-CORE SECURITY

Amni-Core implements security at the systems level through a proprietary computational architecture designed for quantum resilience and GPU-forward execution. The platform employs a novel encoding scheme and memory model that operates fundamentally differently from conventional operating systems. Specific security mechanisms constitute proprietary trade secrets. The platform is designed to provide hardware-level isolation, integrity verification, and resistance to classes of attacks that exploit assumptions inherent in legacy system architectures.
